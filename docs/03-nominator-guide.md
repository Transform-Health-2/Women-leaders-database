# Nominate a Woman Leader — Complete Guide

Welcome! This guide explains how to nominate a woman leader to be featured in the Transform Health Women Leaders Directory.

---

## Why Nominate?

By nominating a leader, you:

✨ **Amplify her visibility** — She'll be discovered by researchers, organizations, and peers worldwide  
🤝 **Strengthen your network** — Recognize someone you know and admire  
🌍 **Increase representation** — Help build a more diverse, inclusive directory  
💪 **Show your support** — A nomination is a powerful endorsement

---

## Who Should You Nominate?

### ✓ Great Candidates for Nomination

- **Women leaders in digital health** — doctors, technologists, policy makers, researchers, entrepreneurs
- **Early-stage leaders** — don't need 20+ years of experience; 3-5 years of meaningful work counts
- **In your network** — colleagues, mentors, advisors, peers you know and respect
- **Making an impact** — even if not widely known, their work matters in digital health
- **Based anywhere** — the directory is global
- **Any role in digital health** — leadership, technical, policy, implementation, research

### Examples of Good Nominations

- A researcher leading an AI project to improve maternal health diagnosis
- A policy officer designing national digital health strategies
- A telemedicine entrepreneur building solutions for rural areas
- A health informatics manager implementing a new hospital system
- A nonprofit director leading digital health initiatives
- An academic studying health data security and privacy

### ⚠ Not a Good Fit

- ✗ Non-leaders (entry-level staff, students, unless exceptional)
- ✗ People who work in healthcare but not in **digital** health (e.g., cardiologist with no tech focus)
- ✗ People who work in non-health sectors (tech, finance, etc. unrelated to health)
- ✗ People who can self-submit easily (they should do it themselves!)
- ✗ Repeat nominators for the same person (check if already nominated)

## How to Nominate — Step by Step

### Step 1: Go to the Directory

Navigate to the Transform Health Women Leaders Directory homepage.

### Step 2: Click "Submit Profile"

You'll see a button labeled **"Submit Profile"** or similar. Click it.

### Step 3: Choose "Nominate a Leader"

You'll see two options:
- "I want to submit my own profile"
- **"I want to nominate a leader"** ← Click this

### Step 4: Fill Out the Nomination Form

The form has these sections:

#### A. About the Nominee

| Field | Required? | What to Enter |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| **First Name** | ✓ | Her first name |
| **Last Name** | ✓ | Her last name |
| **Current Role** | ✓ | Her job title (be specific: "AI Research Lead," not just "Manager") |
| **Organization** | — | Where she works (or "Independent" if self-employed) |
| **LinkedIn Profile URL** | — | Link to her LinkedIn profile (helps us verify her credentials) |

**Examples:**
- Name: Amara Okonkwo
- Role: Head of Digital Health Strategy
- Organization: Ministry of Health, Kenya
- LinkedIn: https://www.linkedin.com/in/amaraokonkwo

#### B. About You (the Nominator)

| Field | Required? | What to Enter | Why We Ask |
|-------|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| **Your Name** | ✓ | Your full name | So we know who nominated her |
| **Your Email** | ✓ | Your contact email | In case we need to verify or ask questions |
| **Your Organization/Role** (optional) | — | Where you work or your role | Context for your nomination |
| **Your Relationship** (optional) | — | How you know her (colleague, mentor, peer, etc.) | Helps us understand the nomination |

#### C. Why You're Nominating Her

| Field | Required? | What to Enter |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| **Why nominate her?** | — | 2-3 sentences about why she deserves to be featured |
| **Additional context** | — | Any other info we should know |

**Good reasons to share:**
- "She's a brilliant AI researcher working on maternal health in rural areas — her work directly impacts policy in 5 African countries."
- "She founded a telemedicine startup that's transformed healthcare access in underserved regions. I've seen the impact firsthand."
- "She's an emerging leader doing innovative work but not as visible as she deserves."
- "She's led digital health transformation at a major hospital system and her insights are invaluable."

### Step 5: Review & Submit

Before submitting:
- [ ] Name is spelled correctly
- [ ] Role is accurate and specific
- [ ] LinkedIn link is correct (if provided)
- [ ] Your contact info is accurate
- [ ] Reason for nomination makes sense

Click **Submit**.

## What Happens After You Nominate?

### Timeline

1. **Immediately:** Nomination goes into our system
2. **Within 3-5 days:** Admin team reviews the nomination
3. **Within 5-7 days:** You receive an email confirmation

### Possible Outcomes

#### ✓ Approved — Profile Goes Live
- Nominated profile appears in the directory with basic info
- Profile shows "Nominated by [Your Name]" as a credit line
- Nominee may be contacted and invited to expand her profile with photo, bio, etc.
- You receive confirmation email

#### ⚠ Pending — More Info Needed
- We may reach out to **you** (the nominator) with questions
- We might ask for clarification on her role, organization, or expertise
- We'll use the email you provided to contact you
- **Action needed:** Respond with more information so we can move forward

#### ✗ Rejected — Not Approved
- We reviewed the nomination and it doesn't fit the criteria
- You'll receive an email explaining why
- Common reasons:
  - Role doesn't align with digital health
  - Information is incomplete or unclear
  - Duplicate (person already in directory)
  - Spam or suspicious submission
- **What you can do:** Provide more info and resubmit, or contact us for clarification

## Multiple Nominations — What to Know

### Can I nominate multiple people?
**Yes!** You can nominate as many people as you like. Each nomination is reviewed separately.

### Should I nominate someone who's already in the directory?
**No.** Before nominating, search the directory to see if she's already listed. If she is, great! Just share her profile with others instead of nominating.

### If the same person is nominated by multiple people
- **That's great!** Multiple nominations strengthen the case
- We'll recognize the first nominator but appreciate all support
- We'll verify the person with the first valid nomination

### Can someone nominate themselves?
- **Technically yes**, but we recommend **self-submitting** instead
- Self-submission gives you full control over your profile
- Self-submitted profiles are often faster to review
- If she's eligible, she should choose "Submit my own profile" instead

## Best Practices for Successful Nominations

### ✓ Do This

1. **Be accurate** — Double-check spelling of name and title
2. **Include LinkedIn** — Helps us verify credentials quickly
3. **Be specific** — "AI researcher" is better than "works in tech"
4. **Explain why** — Give us context on her impact or accomplishments
5. **Nominate leaders you know** — We trust nominations from people who can vouch for accuracy
6. **Check if already nominated** — Search the directory first

### ✗ Don't Do This

1. **Don't nominate strangers** — You should know the person you're nominating
2. **Don't submit spam** — Multiple nominations for non-leaders will be rejected
3. **Don't include false info** — We verify, and false nominations damage trust
4. **Don't nominate people outside digital health** — Keep nominations focused on the mission
5. **Don't nominate people who can self-submit easily** — Let them do it themselves
6. **Don't resubmit the same person multiple times** — One nomination is enough

## Common Questions

### Q: How long does it take for a nomination to be approved?

**A:** Typically 3-5 days for review. If we need more info from you, it may take longer. We'll email you updates.

### Q: What if my nomination is rejected? Can I try again?

**A:** Yes. If rejected, you'll get an explanation. You can:
- Resubmit with more complete information
- Contact us to ask questions
- Suggest the person self-submit instead

### Q: Will the nominee know I nominated her?

**A:** Her profile will show "Nominated by [Your Name]" so yes, she'll know it was you. This is a public credit showing you recommended her. It's meant as recognition!

**If you want to nominate anonymously:** Unfortunately, that's not an option right now. But the nomination is a genuine compliment!

### Q: Do I need to tell her before nominating?

**A:** It's a good idea to let her know you're nominating her, so she's not surprised. But it's not required. She'll see her profile go live and can expand it anytime.

### Q: What if the nominee wants to remove the nomination credit?

**A:** She can contact us and we can update the credit line or remove it. After approval, she has full control over her profile.

### Q: Can I nominate someone who's very new to the field?

**A:** It depends. We look for "leaders" — people with meaningful impact or clear trajectory. Someone with 2-3 years of notable work, published research, or visible projects could qualify. Someone fresh out of school might not (unless exceptional). When in doubt, nominate anyway — we'll review.

### Q: What if I nominate someone but she wants to update her profile later?

**A:** Perfect! After her nomination is approved, she can:
- Search the directory for her name
- Click "Manage Your Profile"
- Expand her nomination with a full bio, photo, expertise tags, etc.
- No re-review needed — updates go live immediately

### Q: Is there a limit to how many people I can nominate?

**A:** No hard limit. Nominate as many as you like. But please only nominate people who genuinely fit the criteria (women leaders in digital health).

### Q: Can I nominate someone from another country?

**A:** **Absolutely!** The directory is global. Nominate leaders from any country, any continent. That's the whole point!
