Open Challenges

Known limitations and risks to address before scale

🔗 LinkedIn — our deliberate approach

LinkedIn has been progressively locking down its platform since 2015. The Profile API was removed from most partners by 2022. In 2023–2024 they doubled down on blocking automated tools and updated their Terms of Service to explicitly prohibit automated data collection. The hiQ v. LinkedIn case (2022) was remanded, leaving the legal status of scraping unresolved and high-risk. Any scraper built today breaks within days.

Our position — form-first, not scrape-first. We treat LinkedIn as a vanity identifier and hyperlink only. The leader fills in their own data through our structured form. This is not a workaround — it is the better approach:

  • Consent-backed data. Every field was submitted with explicit consent. LinkedIn profile data carries no such consent and creates GDPR/privacy exposure.
  • Structured and clean. Our form produces structured fields (expertise tags, years of experience, countries of operation) that LinkedIn free-text bios never would.
  • Platform-independent. If LinkedIn changes again (as Twitter/X did for researchers in 2023), our data is ours. We own the database.
  • Magic link self-service. Leaders can update their own profile without admin involvement via a one-time email link — no LinkedIn dependency required.

Forward looking: As professional networks fragment, having a self-sovereign database of consented, structured leader profiles is a strategic asset. We may explore ORCID (open researcher identity) or Open Badges as verifiable credential layers in a later version — both are open standards that LinkedIn cannot block.

📸 Photo storage — RESOLVED

Photo upload is now fully working. The submit form enforces a mandatory photo, compresses it client-side, and uploads directly to Supabase Storage profile-photos bucket. The photo URL is written to the leader row only on admin approval.

Implementation details:

Supabase Storage
Lives in the same project as the database. One dashboard, one set of credentials, one billing account.

Moderation gate built in
Photo URL is only written to the leaders row on admin approval. No unmoderated images go public.

Client-side compression
Photos compressed before upload. 5MB cap enforced. Faster uploads, less bandwidth.

Free tier sufficient
Supabase free tier includes 1GB storage and 2GB bandwidth. Predictable costs until scale.

User Journey

Complete user flows through the system · Updated May 2026

Public

Public Visitor

Lands on the site and sees the Directory tab — a searchable, filterable grid of leader cards. They can:

  • Search by name, role, or org
  • Filter by continent, country, or expertise via dropdowns
  • Sort by Latest / A–Z / Expertise
  • Load more profiles (pagination)
  • Click a card to open a Profile Modal showing full details: role, org, country, bio, expertise, years of experience, LinkedIn link
  • Switch to the Analytics tab — world map, region tabs with leader counts, specialisation bar chart, click a region to see filtered leader cards below the map

No login, no account. Fully public and read-only.

Self Submit

Leader — Self Submit

  • Hits Submit Profile tab → sees two cards: "I am nominating myself" vs "Nominating someone else" — picks myself
  • Consent gate — must click "Yes I consent" to proceed. Declining ends the flow
  • Basic Info — first name, last name, email (private, not public), profile photo (MANDATORY, 5MB cap, compressed), country of residence, org, role
  • Profile Details — years of experience (pill selector), up to 5 expertise tags, "Other" with free-text, countries of operation (multi-select), bio (300–500 chars enforced)
  • Links & Achievements — LinkedIn URL, up to 3 notable items (title, link, type)
  • Hits Submit Profile → payload written directly to Supabase leaders table via anon insert with status: pending · photo uploads to Supabase Storage profile-photos bucket
  • Sees a thank-you screen: "profile under review, 3–5 business days"
Nominator

Nominator — Submitting Someone Else

  • Same Step 0 but picks "I am nominating someone else"
  • Enters the nominee's LinkedIn or public profile URL
  • Hits Submit Nomination → goes directly to submit, skipping consent/bio/photo steps
  • A minimal row lands on the Sheet: branch: "nominate", nominate_link: <url>, everything else blank
  • Admin sees this in the Nominated tab and uses the pre-built outreach message to contact that person
Manage

Leader — Managing Their Profile

Already in the directory and wants to update or remove their listing:

  • Clicks "Manage or remove your profile" on the Submit page (or the button in their Profile Modal)
  • Identify step — enters first name, last name, email, optional LinkedIn
  • Hits "Find my profile" → calls the live Sheet, matches by exact first + last name, shows a "Profile found" card with their role and org
  • Two paths:
    • Update path: Picks "Update profile" → free-text box describing what to change → submits → profileRequest saved to Requests sheet, status pending
    • Delete path: Picks "Remove profile" → optional reason field → submits → same profileRequest row, request_type: delete
Admin

Admin

Navigates to the Admin console (separate route, not in public nav). Currently in test mode — no login required. Auth will be re-enabled before launch.

  • Pending Submissions tab — all self-submitted profiles awaiting review. Searchable + filterable by country, expertise, sort order. Expand any row to see full bio, profile details, email (private), LinkedIn. Approve → sets status: live → appears in public directory. Reject → sets status: rejected.
  • Nominated tab — nomination-only submissions (just a LinkedIn URL). Expand to see nominee details and a pre-built outreach message. Copy message to clipboard → reach out manually. Approve or reject once the person has been contacted and confirmed.
  • Profile Requests tab — update and delete requests from existing leaders.
    • Updates sub-tab — leader has described what they want changed; admin clicks "Send update link via email" → magic link goes to their inbox for self-service editing
    • Deletes sub-tab — leader wants to be removed; admin checks boxes and approves → profile set to rejected and removed from public directory
  • All Entries tab — full table of every record (live, pending, rejected) with search, sort, pagination, expandable detail rows.
  • Summary stats (Pending / Live / Rejected counts) always visible at the top.
Flow

Where we are and why this is the right move

We built fast with Google Sheets + Apps Script and got 81 leaders into a working directory. Then we hit a wall: Firebase Storage never got off the ground, Apps Script requires a manual redeploy every time we change a line of backend code, there's no real admin login, and the magic link system is entirely hand-rolled. We are not migrating from a complete stack — we are completing it for the first time.

Supabase is the single platform that closes every open gap.

Pain point today With Supabase
Every Code.gs change needs a manual "New version" redeploy on Google's side No deploy step — schema and functions update instantly
Admin login button is literally disabled — no real auth exists Supabase Auth gives proper email/password login in <1 hour
Magic link flow built from scratch — token, Requests sheet, MailApp, mark-used logic supabase.auth.signInWithOtp({ email }) — one line, production-grade
Reads all 81+ records to filter by status (no server-side query) SQL: SELECT * FROM leaders WHERE status = 'live' with an index
Firebase Storage + Apps Script + Google Sheets = 3 separate services Database + storage + auth + edge functions in one platform
expertise, countries, notableItems stored as comma strings / JSON blobs in cells Proper columns, text[] arrays, or a related table with full query support
Admin must hit Refresh to see new submissions Supabase Realtime — new submissions appear in the queue live
Apps Script 6-minute execution limit and daily quota No execution limits on database queries

  • The entire React frontend — all pages, components, and UI
  • GitHub Pages deployment (or optionally move to Vercel for cleaner env var handling)
  • The submit form, admin console, analytics page, and directory — no UX changes
  • The 81 leaders — migrated via a one-time script, no data loss

Identity strategy — beyond LinkedIn

🔭 Forward-thinking approach to professional identity

LinkedIn's API restrictions are getting tighter, not looser. Since 2022 they have removed profile access from most partners, blocked the majority of OAuth scopes, and updated their ToS to explicitly ban automated data collection. Treating LinkedIn as anything more than a hyperlink is a liability.

Our current position is already the right one. LinkedIn URL = a public link to verify who someone is. Nothing more. All structured data lives in our own database, submitted with explicit consent.

What we can build toward:

  • ORCID integration. ORCID is an open, non-profit researcher identity standard used by 20M+ researchers globally. It has a real API, no scraping needed, and is explicitly designed for exactly this kind of directory. Worth exploring as an optional "verify via ORCID" button for leaders with academic or research backgrounds.
  • Open Badges / Verifiable Credentials. Emerging W3C standard. Leaders could carry a "Transform Health Verified" credential in a digital wallet, portable across platforms — not locked to LinkedIn at all.
  • Email-as-identity. The magic link pattern we already have is effectively passwordless, self-sovereign identity. When Supabase Auth handles this, it becomes production-grade with no extra work.
  • Our data is our moat. A database of 81 (and growing) consented, structured, verified leader profiles is something LinkedIn cannot replicate — it is curated, purposeful, and consent-backed. That is the asset. LinkedIn is just a link.

Summary: Supabase migration + email OTP auth puts us in a position where professional identity is handled cleanly, without any dependency on platforms we do not control.
